Each word will have it's own page with extra information about inflected forms and, where possible, etymology. Etymologies of words are found by looking up the etymology of cognates in Scots on this page and DOST, or by comparing Lanwurq words to glossaries of Old and Middle Scots. Since the infinitive of all verbs is marked with the prefix qe-, each verb entry will show the bare verb to avoid all verbs being listed under the Q page. Some words tend to appear mostly in the definite forms, this is true especially for language names. In these cases these words will be sorted according to the letter that follows the definite article i- which will be placed in brackets. So for example "Danish language" is listed as (i)deñslíd under the D section. There is a very large amount of words in the C page beginning with the ability prefix cn- due to it being able to derives nouns and adjectives of ability from any verb. Affixes and clitics are listed separately on this page.
