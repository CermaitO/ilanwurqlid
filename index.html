I L A N W U R Q L Í D

Líd/Language:

Híar iwijq aneñq ilanwurqlíts gremur in wurts juocnsích.
Ilanwurqlíts afij wístdjírmañslíd þiqs spichiq in aphuchuo eichlahhns in Focurclanwurq, awí éarq in Focurc in is'chóqs léhôns. 'Sdúñchmn fe ioodngoñslíd ben iootscóqslíd in imitscóqslíd se 'ssibhiq qe is'chótslíd. 'Sadód eianglisfríscheslíts fémle þiq ijólaslíd, ifngajnslíd, i-ngoñslíd in ifríscheslíd iqhus ano. Se iiður líts fe Scóqôn, ilanwurqlíts bín trañqebhi cioq se þiq i-ngoñslíd ébdíd spichiq réður, in is afénin iqwurciq fur þiq ilanwurqlíts aséar dín líd we énle aphuchuo eisphichurs þiqr fóon nú.

Lanwurq is a moribund West Germanic language that is spoken in a few villages in Focurclanwurt, a small region in Falkirk in the Scottish Lowlands. It descends from Old English via Old and Middle Scots and so it is related to Scots. It is part of the Anglo-Frisian languages which also has Yola, Fingalian, English and Frisian. Like other languages from Scotland, Lanwurq has faced attempts to kill it so that everyone would speak English instead, and this attempt worked because now Lanwurq is a dying language with only a few speakers remaining.
